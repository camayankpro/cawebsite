1. What is GST LUT and How Does It Work for Nagapattinam Exporters?

Under GST, exports of goods and services are zero-rated supplies вЂ” meaning the GST rate on exports is 0%. However, to export at 0% without paying any GST upfront, you must either:

  1. File a Letter of Undertaking (LUT) вЂ” a formal declaration on the GST portal promising that you will fulfil all export obligations without paying IGST; OR
  2. Execute a Bond вЂ” a legal bond (with or without a bank guarantee) committing to pay IGST + penalty if the export conditions are not met; OR
  3. Pay IGST and claim a refund вЂ” export with IGST payment and file a GST refund claim after the export is completed.

For virtually all regular exporters in Nagapattinam, LUT is the best option вЂ” it is free, renewable annually, does not require a bank guarantee, and eliminates all IGST cash outflow on exports.

How the LUT mechanism works in practice:

  • You file the LUT on the GST portal for the current financial year вЂ” TaxAMC does this in April
  • Your LUT reference number is noted вЂ” this must be quoted on all export invoices during the year
  • You raise a zero-rated export invoice to the foreign buyer with 0% IGST вЂ” the LUT reference number is mentioned
  • Foreign buyer pays in foreign currency вЂ” you receive USD/GBP/EUR in your bank account
  • No IGST is collected, no IGST is paid, no refund needs to be claimed вЂ” zero GST cash flow impact
  • The export is declared in GSTR-1 under the "Exports with LUT" category вЂ” no GST payment in GSTR-3B for these transactions

7. Annual LUT Renewal вЂ” Why It Cannot Be Missed by Nagapattinam Exporters

LUT is valid for exactly one financial year вЂ” 1 April to 31 March. A new LUT must be filed at the start of every financial year before raising the first export invoice of that year.

Consequences of exporting without a valid (renewed) LUT:

  • Invoices raised after March 31st without a new LUT are technically non-zero-rated exports
  • IGST can be demanded on all such invoices вЂ” with 18% interest from the invoice date
  • Export incentive claims (RoDTEP, drawback) may be rejected for invoices without valid LUT

8. What If You Exported Without LUT вЂ” IGST Refund Process in Nagapattinam

If you have already made exports from Nagapattinam without filing an LUT (and with IGST payment), you can claim a GST refund of the IGST paid. However, the refund process is significantly more complex and time-consuming than simply having an LUT in place.

IGST Refund process for Nagapattinam exporters:

  • Automatic route (goods exports): IGST paid on export invoices is automatically processed for refund by the GST system after the shipping bill is filed at customs вЂ” typically 30вЂ“60 days if all data matches
  • Manual route (service exports): Service exporters must file a formal refund application on the GST portal (Form GST RFD-01) with supporting documents
  • Bank realisation requirement: Foreign payment must be received in the bank and confirmed with FIRC before the refund is processed
  • Time limit for refund claim: Application must be filed within 2 years of the relevant GST return due date
  • Common refund delays: Mismatches between GSTR-1 export data and shipping bill data, FIRC not submitted, bank realisation not complete, officer queries on export documentation

📄 Documents Required for GST Refund of IGST Paid on Exports

  • Export invoices вЂ” all invoices on which IGST was paid and for which refund is claimed.
  • GSTR-3B of the relevant months вЂ” showing IGST paid on exports.
  • Shipping bill (goods exports) вЂ” customs document confirming export has taken place.
  • FIRC / Bank Realisation Certificate вЂ” confirming receipt of foreign currency payment.
  • Bank statement вЂ” showing receipt of foreign remittance correlated to each invoice.
  • Export contract / purchase order вЂ” supporting the genuineness of the export transaction.
  • Statement of invoices вЂ” summary of all export invoices for which refund is claimed (in prescribed format).
