1. What is ROC Annual Filing and Why Does It Matter for Patna Companies?

ROC Annual Filing is the mandatory submission of financial statements and annual return to the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) every year. It applies to every registered company and LLP — including those with zero revenue or no transactions during the year.

Why ROC filing matters for your Patna business:

  • Maintains your company's Active status on the MCA registry — required for all banking, contracts, and government tenders
  • Keeps directors' DIN (Director Identification Number) valid and active
  • Provides legal evidence of financial health to banks, investors, and suppliers
  • Required before making any changes to the company — directors, address, or share capital
  • Mandatory for all loan applications, government tenders, and export registrations
  • Protects directors from disqualification under Section 164(2) of the Companies Act

⚠️ A company that fails to file ROC returns for 2+ consecutive years gets struck off by MCA. Once struck off, all bank accounts are frozen, contracts become unenforceable, and revival requires a lengthy NCLT process. TaxAMC prevents this for every company we manage in Patna.

4. Director Disqualification — What Every Patna Business Owner Must Know

Under Section 164(2) of the Companies Act, a director is disqualified for 5 years if the company fails to file ROC annual returns for 3 consecutive years. A disqualified director:

  • Cannot be appointed or continue as a director in any company in India for 5 years
  • Cannot sign any MCA forms or filings during the disqualification period
  • Has their disqualification listed publicly on the MCA website — visible to banks, investors, and partners
  • Faces difficulty opening business bank accounts or obtaining loans
  • Cannot easily invest in or participate in any new company
