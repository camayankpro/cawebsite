3. Industry-Specific GST Issues for Toy Manufacturers & Traders

Toy Manufacturers & Traders businesses regularly face these specific GST compliance challenges:

  • Import vs domestic GST differential on toys
  • BIS certification compliance for toy manufacturers
  • HSN classification for electronic vs non-electronic toys
  • ITC on plastic and electronic components
  • Export of Indian toys — PLI scheme compliance

Each of these issues requires careful handling. Wrong classification, missed ITC, or delayed returns can result in significant financial exposure. TaxAMC's CA team is experienced in handling these industry-specific challenges for Toy Manufacturers & Traders businesses across India.

4. ITR Filing for Toys Business Owners

Toy Manufacturers & Traders business owners must file ITR annually. The form depends on business structure:

StructureITR FormDeadline FY 2025-26
Proprietorship (presumptive, turnover < ₹2 crore)ITR-431st July 2026
Proprietorship (regular books)ITR-331st July or 31st October 2026
Partnership FirmITR-531st October 2026
Private Limited CompanyITR-631st October 2026

Tax Audit: Mandatory if turnover exceeds ₹1 crore (₹10 crore for fully digital transactions) or if declaring income below 8% of turnover under presumptive taxation. TaxAMC handles complete tax audit and ITR for Toy Manufacturers & Traders businesses.

Advance Tax: Toy Manufacturers & Traders business owners must pay advance tax in four instalments — 15th June, 15th September, 15th December, and 15th March. Missing instalments attracts 1% monthly interest under Sections 234B and 234C.

5. Tax Deductions Toy Manufacturers & Traders Businesses Can Claim

Maximising legitimate deductions significantly reduces taxable income. Key deductions for Toy Manufacturers & Traders businesses:

  • Raw material costs — plastic, metal, fabric
  • Mould and tool depreciation
  • BIS certification and testing costs
  • Advertising and packaging
  • Import duty on specific components

Additionally, all Toy Manufacturers & Traders businesses can claim: Standard Deduction under Section 16 (for proprietors drawing salary), Section 80C up to ₹1.5 lakh (for individual proprietors), health insurance under 80D, and NPS contribution under 80CCD(1B). Toy Manufacturers & Traders businesses making payments to contractors, workers, or professionals must deduct TDS:

TDS must be deposited by 7th of the following month. Quarterly TDS returns (26Q) must be filed on time. Non-deduction attracts 1% per month interest and disallowance of the expense in ITR.
