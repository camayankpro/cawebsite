A salary slip (payslip) is a formal document issued by an employer to every employee each month — detailing gross earnings, statutory deductions, and net take-home pay. It is not just a courtesy document; it is a legally significant record that employees need for loan applications, tax filing, visa processing, and job changes. For employers, it is proof of salary payment and compliance with TDS and other statutory obligations. This guide explains what a proper salary slip must contain and how to issue them correctly.

1. Mandatory Components of a Salary Slip

Earnings Section:

  • Basic Salary — taxable; basis for PF, gratuity, and leave encashment calculation
  • HRA (House Rent Allowance) — partially exempt if employee pays rent and meets conditions
  • Conveyance / Transport Allowance — taxable (standard deduction covers this under new regime)
  • Special Allowance — fully taxable, balancing component of CTC
  • Medical Allowance — taxable (tax-free reimbursement of actual bills is more efficient)
  • LTA (Leave Travel Allowance) — exempt for actual travel costs twice in a 4-year block
  • Bonus / Incentive — fully taxable in month paid
  • Overtime Pay — taxable

Deductions Section:

  • TDS (Tax Deducted at Source — Section 192) — based on estimated annual taxable income
  • EPF Employee Contribution — 12% of Basic + DA
  • ESI Employee Contribution — 0.75% of gross wages (for eligible employees)
  • Professional Tax — as per state slab (applicable states only)
  • Loan / Advance Recovery — any advances given to employee
  • Voluntary PF — if employee opts for VPF above 12%

Net Pay = Gross Earnings − Total Deductions

2. Why Salary Slips Matter

  • Home / vehicle loan applications: Banks require 3–6 months of salary slips as income proof
  • Visa applications: Embassies require salary slips as proof of employment and financial stability
  • Income Tax Return filing: Employees use salary slips to verify TDS deducted and claim correct deductions
  • Job change negotiations: New employers use current salary slips to determine offer package
  • Employee loan from employer: Salary slip documents the base for recovery
  • Legal disputes: Salary slips are primary evidence in labour disputes, wrongful termination cases, and PF claims

3. Employer Obligations Regarding Salary Slips

  • Provide salary slip to every employee every month without exception — email PDF is acceptable
  • Salary slip must accurately reflect all components — inflating CTC on paper while paying less is a labour law violation
  • TDS deducted on salary slip must match TDS deposited with the government — verified annually via Form 26AS
  • PF and ESI deductions on payslip must match amounts deposited with EPFO and ESIC
  • Maintain payroll records for at least 8 years — required for PF and income tax audits

4. Digital Salary Slips and Password Protection

Most businesses now issue password-protected PDF salary slips by email — this is acceptable and preferred. The standard password convention is the employee's date of birth (DDMMYYYY) — ensuring only the employee can open it. For loans and visa, banks/embassies accept digital salary slips with company letterhead and authorised signature (digital or wet ink).
