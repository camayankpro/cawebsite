1. What is IEC and Who Needs It in Jorhat?

The Import Export Code is issued by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) under the Ministry of Commerce. It is a mandatory registration for every business or individual that imports or exports goods from India.

You need an IEC if your Jorhat business:

  • Imports any goods — raw materials, equipment, finished products, electronics, machinery
  • Exports goods — textiles, handicrafts, engineering products, FMCG, chemicals, pharmaceuticals
  • Sells products on international e-commerce platforms — Amazon Global, Etsy, eBay, Shopify
  • Exports services and wants to file a GST LUT for zero-rated export invoicing
  • Receives foreign remittances via banking channels for exported services
  • Plans to participate in international trade fairs or exhibitions
  • Wants to claim export incentives — RoDTEP, MEIS, advance authorisation, EPCG

⚠️ Without IEC, customs will not release your imported goods. Goods held at customs accumulate demurrage charges of thousands of rupees per day. If your Jorhat business is planning imports, get your IEC before placing the first order — not after.

5. IEC + GST LUT — Export Without Paying IGST Upfront

One of the highest-value combinations for Jorhat exporters is IEC + GST LUT (Letter of Undertaking). With a valid LUT, your exports are zero-rated — you raise export invoices without collecting or paying IGST, significantly improving cash flow.

  • Without LUT: Pay IGST on exports upfront → claim refund later — tying up capital for months
  • With LUT: Export invoices are zero-rated — no IGST payment, no waiting for refunds
  • LUT must be filed at the start of each financial year (April) — TaxAMC files your annual LUT as part of the package
  • Applies to both goods exports and service exports including software, consulting, and digital services

6. Annual IEC Update — Keeping Your IEC Active

IEC has lifetime validity but requires a mandatory annual update/confirmation between April and June every year. Businesses that miss the annual update face IEC deactivation — which blocks all customs clearances and foreign payment processing until the update is completed.
