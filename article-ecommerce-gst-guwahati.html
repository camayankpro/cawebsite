1. Why GST is Absolutely Mandatory for All Online Sellers in Guwahati — No Exceptions

Under Section 24 of the CGST Act, 2017, e-commerce sellers are mandatorily required to register for GST regardless of their annual turnover. The standard threshold exemption of ₹20 lakh (services) or ₹40 lakh (goods) that protects small physical businesses does not apply to any seller on any e-commerce marketplace.

Why this rule exists: E-commerce platforms operate across all states — your goods sold on Amazon ship to buyers in Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, and everywhere else. This makes all your sales "inter-state supplies" — which mandatorily require GST registration regardless of turnover.

Consequences of selling on Amazon/Flipkart without GST in Guwahati:

  • Platform account suspension: Amazon and Flipkart periodically verify seller GSTIN compliance and suspend non-compliant accounts — often without warning
  • Lost TCS: Amazon/Flipkart collect 1% TCS on all your sales. Without a GSTIN, this cannot be credited to your account — it becomes a permanent loss. On ₹10 lakh in sales, that's ₹10,000 lost
  • Backdated GST demand: GST authorities can demand tax on all sales made since your first marketplace sale — with 18% interest from each sale date
  • Penalty: 10% of tax due (minimum ₹10,000) for not obtaining mandatory registration
  • Criminal liability: In extreme cases, suppression of liability above ₹2 crore can result in arrest

3. TCS — Tax Collected by Amazon and Flipkart — Don't Lose This Money

E-commerce operators (Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, etc.) are required under Section 52 of the CGST Act to collect 1% TCS on the net taxable value of all sales made through their platform and deposit it to the government in your name.

How TCS works for your Guwahati business:

  • Amazon/Flipkart collects 1% from the purchase amount before paying you — so for a ₹1,000 sale, they deposit ₹10 as TCS on your behalf
  • This TCS is deposited using your GSTIN — it appears in your GSTR-2B every month as available credit
  • You claim this TCS credit in GSTR-3B — it reduces your net GST payable or creates a refund
  • If you do NOT claim TCS credit in your GSTR-3B, it accumulates in the GST portal but does not automatically get refunded — you must claim it explicitly in every GSTR-3B

5. Amazon FBA and Multi-State GST — What Guwahati Sellers Must Know

Amazon FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) stores your inventory in Amazon's warehouses across India. When you send stock to an Amazon fulfillment centre in, say, Mumbai or Bengaluru, you are making an inter-state stock transfer — which is a GST taxable supply even though you haven't sold anything yet.

FBA GST obligations for Guwahati sellers:

  • Stock transfer to FBA warehouse in another state = Supply under GST: You must issue a stock transfer invoice with IGST to move inventory between states
  • Registration in each state where FBA warehouse is located: Technically required under GST law — but Amazon has a special FBA consignment arrangement that TaxAMC advises on
  • ISD (Input Service Distributor) mechanism: If registered in multiple states, ITC on common services must be distributed through ISD — complex compliance that TaxAMC manages
  • Practical reality: Most small FBA sellers in Guwahati operate with a single Assam GSTIN — TaxAMC advises on your specific turnover and warehouse combination to determine actual compliance requirement

6. Swiggy, Zomato, and Food Delivery — Special GST Rules Since January 2022

Since 1st January 2022, food delivery aggregators (Swiggy, Zomato, etc.) are responsible for collecting and paying GST on restaurant supplies made through their platform. This means:

  • If you are a restaurant selling on Swiggy/Zomato — you do NOT collect or pay GST on aggregator orders. The aggregator does it.
  • Your GSTR-1 must separately report Swiggy/Zomato sales in the "Supplies through e-commerce operator" table
  • Do NOT pay GST separately on Swiggy/Zomato orders — you will double-pay. TaxAMC checks this common error for all food seller clients
  • Direct sales (phone orders, walk-ins) still attract 5% GST which you collect and pay normally

7. Income Tax (ITR) for E-Commerce Sellers in Guwahati

In addition to GST, all e-commerce sellers must file annual Income Tax Returns. Key ITR rules for marketplace sellers:

  • ITR form: Proprietors use ITR-3 (business income). Companies use ITR-6. LLPs use ITR-5. Partnerships use ITR-5.
  • Income head: All marketplace sales declared under "Profits and Gains from Business and Profession"
  • Business expenses deductible: Product cost, marketplace fees (Amazon commission, Flipkart selling fees), packaging, shipping, storage, advertising spend, returns
  • TCS is NOT income — it's advance tax: The 1% TCS collected by Amazon/Flipkart must be claimed as advance tax in your ITR — it reduces your total tax payable
  • Amazon payout statements: TaxAMC uses your Amazon seller account payout reports to reconcile total income, returns, commissions, and advertising spend for accurate ITR preparation
