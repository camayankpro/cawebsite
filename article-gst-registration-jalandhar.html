1. Who Must Register for GST in Jalandhar?

As per the GST Act, 2017, the following businesses in Jalandhar must mandatorily register for GST:

  • Turnover threshold: Annual turnover above ₹40 lakh for goods suppliers; above ₹20 lakh for service providers in Punjab
  • Interstate suppliers: Jalandhar businesses supplying goods or services to other states — regardless of turnover
  • E-commerce sellers: All sellers on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, or any online marketplace — mandatory regardless of annual turnover
  • Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM): Businesses receiving specified supplies on which they must pay GST under RCM
  • Importers and exporters: Any Jalandhar business engaged in cross-border trade of goods or services
  • Casual taxable persons: Those making occasional taxable supplies in or from Jalandhar
  • Agents of registered suppliers operating in Jalandhar

Even below the threshold, voluntary GST registration is advisable for B2B businesses in Jalandhar — unregistered suppliers cannot issue tax invoices, which blocks buyers' ITC claims and frequently costs businesses their largest client contracts.

3. Documents Required for GST Registration in Jalandhar

  • Sole Proprietor / Individual: PAN card, Aadhaar card (mobile linked), passport photograph, business address proof (electricity bill / rent agreement / NOC), cancelled cheque or bank statement
  • Partnership Firm / LLP: PAN of firm and all partners, Partnership Deed / LLP Agreement, designated partner authorization, business address proof in Jalandhar

7. Penalty for Non-Registration

Operating without GST registration when mandatory attracts a penalty of 10% of tax due (minimum ₹10,000). For deliberate tax evasion, the penalty rises to 100% of the tax amount. Beyond the financial penalty, Jalandhar businesses operating without a GSTIN regularly lose their largest B2B clients — buyers cannot claim ITC on unregistered supplier invoices.

⚠️ Important for Jalandhar businesses: GST registration is required before your first taxable supply — not after crossing the turnover threshold. Retrospective registration demands include penalties and interest on all past taxable supplies. Register early — it is free and opens significant business opportunities.
