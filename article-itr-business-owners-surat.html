1. Business ITR Overview for Surat

Surat's business landscape — Diamond cutting and polishing (90% of world's diamonds), Synthetic textiles and fabric, Petrochemicals (Hazira) — means businesses here have diverse tax profiles. Key decisions before filing ITR for FY 2025-26:

  • Do you qualify for presumptive taxation under Section 44AD or 44ADA?
  • Is your turnover above the tax audit threshold (₹1 crore for businesses)?
  • Are you maintaining proper books of accounts?
  • Have you paid advance tax in four instalments?
  • Does your ITR turnover match your GST annual return?

2. ITR-3 vs ITR-4 for Surat Business Owners

FormWho Should Use
ITR-4 (Sugam)Businesses in Surat opting for presumptive taxation — turnover up to ₹2 crore (Section 44AD) or professional receipts up to ₹75 lakh (Section 44ADA) — with no capital gains or foreign assets
ITR-3Regular accounting — turnover above audit limits, businesses with capital gains, those not eligible for presumptive taxation, or those maintaining full books

3. Presumptive Taxation Under Section 44AD for Surat Businesses

Small traders and manufacturers in Surat with turnover up to ₹2 crore can opt for Section 44AD presumptive taxation — declaring 8% of gross turnover as income (6% for fully digital receipts) without maintaining detailed books. This is ideal for small businesses in Surat's Diamond cutting and polishing (90% of world's diamonds) sector that want to minimise compliance burden.

Section 44ADA for professionals: Doctors, lawyers, engineers, architects, and consultants in Surat with gross receipts up to ₹75 lakh can declare 50% as presumptive income under 44ADA.

Important restriction: If you opt out of presumptive in any year, you cannot use it for the next 5 years. Our CA team evaluates whether presumptive is sustainable for your Surat business before recommending it.

5. Business Deductions Surat Business Owners Can Claim

  • Rent for business premises in Surat
  • Salaries and wages paid to employees
  • Electricity, telephone, and internet bills
  • Depreciation on assets — machinery, vehicles, computers — under Section 32
  • Interest on business loans
  • Raw material and stock purchases

Not deductible: Personal expenses, cash payments above ₹10,000 per day to a single person in some cases, penalties and fines.
