A Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is an electronic equivalent of a physical signature — legally valid, secure, and required for filing income tax returns, GST applications, MCA (ROC) forms, import-export transactions, tender submissions, and many other government and business processes. If you run a company, are a director, or deal in government procurement — a Class 3 DSC is not optional. This guide explains DSC types, applicability, and the process to obtain one.

1. Types of DSC in India

ClassUseWho Needs It
Class 3 DSCMost secure — for high-value, high-risk transactionsCompany directors (MCA filings), income tax returns (companies), GST applications, e-tendering, import-export (IEC), trademark filing, DGFT
Document Signer DSCFor organisations signing large volumes of documents (invoices, certificates)Organisations sending bulk e-documents — auto-signing
DGFT DSCSpecific to DGFT (Directorate General of Foreign Trade) transactionsImporters and exporters for DGFT portal filings

Class 1 and Class 2 DSCs are no longer issued by certifying authorities — only Class 3 (the most secure) is issued for new applications.

2. When is a DSC Required?

  • Income Tax: Companies and LLPs must e-verify / sign ITR using DSC (not Aadhaar OTP)
  • MCA / ROC filings: All e-forms filed on the MCA portal — company incorporation (SPICe+), annual filings (AOC-4, MGT-7), director appointment (DIR-12), and all other forms require DSC of a director
  • GST: Company and LLP GST registrations, amendments, and refund applications require DSC
  • E-tendering: Government procurement tenders (GeM, CPPP, and state portals) require Class 3 DSC for bid submission
  • DGFT / IEC: Import Export Code applications and DGFT filings
  • IP India: Trademark and patent applications filed online require DSC
  • EPFO: PF e-sign for employer filings

A company director without a valid DSC cannot sign any MCA form — blocking annual filings and any corporate action. Renew your DSC before it expires.

3. DSC Application Process

  • Step 1: Choose a licensed Certifying Authority (CA) — e-Mudhra, Sify, Capricorn, NSDL, or others
  • Step 2: Fill online application — select DSC type (Individual / Organisation / DGFT), validity period (1 or 2 years), and token type
  • Step 4: Video verification (live — applicant appears on video call with the certifying authority's officer for identity verification)
  • Step 5: DSC issued and stored on a secure USB token (dongle) — or as a software-based DSC
  • Timeline: 1–2 working days for most certifying authorities after video verification

4. DSC Validity and Renewal

A DSC is valid for 1 or 2 years from the date of issue. Renewal must be done before expiry — an expired DSC cannot be used for any digital signing. When a DSC expires during an important filing period (e.g., ROC annual filing season), it can block critical compliance. Set a calendar reminder 1 month before expiry.
