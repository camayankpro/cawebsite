2. Key Eligibility Factors Banks Check

  • Business vintage: Most banks prefer 2â€“3 years of operations. New businesses should approach MUDRA, CGTMSE, or SIDBI instead.
  • ITR-based income: Banks assess income based on ITR â€” income shown in ITR = income you can borrow against. Low income in ITR = low loan eligibility. This is the single most common reason for lower-than-needed sanctions.
  • CIBIL score: Promoter personal CIBIL should be 700+; company CIBIL (CMR rating) should be healthy. Clean repayment history is critical.
  • GST turnover: Banks cross-verify your turnover against GST returns â€” GSTR-3B turnover should match financial statements
  • Bank account activity: Healthy current account transactions with regular credits and debits. Cash deposits raise red flags for banks.
  • Existing debt: DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) = Net Cash Accrual / Total Debt Repayment obligation â€” banks want DSCR > 1.25
  • Collateral: For secured loans â€” property value must cover loan amount with adequate margin (typically loan = 60â€“75% of property value)

3. Common Mistakes That Lead to Loan Rejection

  • Low income in ITR: Years of minimising taxable income means the bank sees a low-income business unworthy of a large loan
  • Mismatch between GST turnover and financial statements: Bank computers flag this and it triggers manual scrutiny
  • Approaching the wrong bank: PSU banks like SBI have different (usually easier) criteria for MSME loans versus private banks. Some banks specialise in your industry.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing ITR computation, no GSTR reconciliation, outdated balance sheets
  • Poor current account behaviour: Cheque bounces, irregular deposits, no business transactions in account
  • Multiple recent credit enquiries: Too many simultaneous loan applications damage CIBIL score
