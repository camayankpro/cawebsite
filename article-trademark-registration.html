Trademark Registration is the most important step in protecting your brand — your business name, logo, tagline, or product name. A registered trademark gives you the exclusive legal right to use that mark in commerce, the ability to take legal action against infringers, and the ® symbol that signals serious brand ownership. In India, trademarks are registered under the Trade Marks Act, 1999 — and the process, while straightforward, requires careful class selection and professional handling to be effective. This guide explains trademark registration from search to certificate.

1. What Can Be Registered as a Trademark?

  • Brand names / word marks: "Tata", "Amul", your company name, product name
  • Logos and device marks: The visual logo of your brand
  • Taglines / slogans: "Just Do It", "Connecting People"
  • Combination marks: Name + logo together
  • 3D shapes: Distinctive product shape or packaging (e.g., Coca-Cola bottle)
  • Colours: Specific colour combinations that uniquely identify a brand
  • Sound marks: Distinctive jingles or sound branding

What cannot be registered: Descriptive words (e.g., "Fresh" for a fruit seller), generic terms, deceptive marks, marks identical to existing registered trademarks, national flags, and government emblems.

2. Trademark Classes — Choosing the Right Class(es)

India follows the Nice Classification — 45 classes of goods and services. You must apply in every class that covers your business activities. Filing in the wrong class provides no protection for your actual business:

ClassesCategoryExamples
1–34GoodsFood (29,30), Clothing (25), Electronics (9), Pharmaceuticals (5), Beverages (32,33)
35–45ServicesAdvertising/Business (35), Finance/Insurance (36), IT/Software (42), Education (41), Legal (45), Healthcare (44)

Most businesses need 1–3 classes. Filing in multiple classes requires separate fees per class but provides broader protection.

Wrong class selection = zero protection. Many applicants file in the wrong class and discover the mistake only when infringement occurs. Get professional class selection advice.

4. Protection a Registered Trademark Gives You

  • Exclusive right to use the mark in commerce for registered goods/services
  • Right to sue for trademark infringement (civil and criminal remedies)
  • Customs can block import of counterfeit goods bearing your mark
  • Basis for international trademark protection (Madrid Protocol)
  • Asset value — trademarks can be licensed, franchised, or sold
  • 10-year validity, renewable indefinitely every 10 years

Using a name for years without registration doesn't protect you — a newcomer can register it and stop you from using your own brand. Register before someone else does.
