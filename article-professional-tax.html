1. Where Does Professional Tax Apply?

Professional Tax is levied by state governments â€” not all states have it. States where PT currently applies include: Maharashtra, Karnataka, West Bengal, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Assam, Chhattisgarh, Kerala, Meghalaya, Orissa, Bihar, Jharkhand, and Sikkim. States like Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, Haryana, Punjab, and Himachal Pradesh do not levy Professional Tax.

3. Employer Obligations under Professional Tax

  • Employer Registration: Every employer must register with the state PT authority â€” separate from employee registration. Penalties for late registration.
  • Employee Registration: Each employee earning above the exempt threshold must be registered. PT is deducted from their salary each month.
  • Monthly Deduction: Deduct PT from eligible employees' salaries each month as per state slab rates
  • Monthly Remittance: Pay the deducted PT to the state government by the due date (usually 20th of the following month)
  • Annual Return / Enrollment: File annual PT return as per state requirement
  • PT on self-employed / proprietors: Proprietors and self-employed individuals must register and pay PT on their own income from profession/trade

4. Income Tax Deduction for PT Paid

Professional Tax paid by an employee is fully deductible under Section 16(iii) of the Income Tax Act â€” reducing the taxable income in the employee's hands. Employers deduct PT from salary, deposit it with the state, and reflect it in Form 16. This is one of the standard deductions from gross salary before computing income tax.
