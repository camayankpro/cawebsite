1. Business ITR Overview for Kavaratti

Kavaratti's business landscape — Tourism and eco-tourism, Fishing and seafood exports, Coir products — means businesses here have diverse tax profiles. Key decisions before filing ITR for FY 2025-26:

  • Do you qualify for presumptive taxation under Section 44AD or 44ADA?
  • Is your turnover above the tax audit threshold (₹1 crore for businesses)?
  • Are you maintaining proper books of accounts?
  • Have you paid advance tax in four instalments?
  • Does your ITR turnover match your GST annual return?

2. ITR-3 vs ITR-4 for Kavaratti Business Owners

FormWho Should Use
ITR-4 (Sugam)Businesses in Kavaratti opting for presumptive taxation — turnover up to ₹2 crore (Section 44AD) or professional receipts up to ₹75 lakh (Section 44ADA) — with no capital gains or foreign assets
ITR-3Regular accounting — turnover above audit limits, businesses with capital gains, those not eligible for presumptive taxation, or those maintaining full books

3. Presumptive Taxation Under Section 44AD for Kavaratti Businesses

Small traders and manufacturers in Kavaratti with turnover up to ₹2 crore can opt for Section 44AD presumptive taxation — declaring 8% of gross turnover as income (6% for fully digital receipts) without maintaining detailed books. This is ideal for small businesses in Kavaratti's Tourism and eco-tourism sector that want to minimise compliance burden.

Section 44ADA for professionals: Doctors, lawyers, engineers, architects, and consultants in Kavaratti with gross receipts up to ₹75 lakh can declare 50% as presumptive income under 44ADA.

Important restriction: If you opt out of presumptive in any year, you cannot use it for the next 5 years. Our CA team evaluates whether presumptive is sustainable for your Kavaratti business before recommending it.
