1. When Should You Cancel Your GST Registration in Navsari?

GST cancellation is the legally correct action in these situations:

  • Business closure: Your Navsari business has shut down and you will not make taxable supplies going forward
  • Below threshold turnover: Your annual aggregate turnover has fallen below ₹20 lakh (services) or ₹40 lakh (goods) and you no longer wish to be voluntarily registered
  • Business sale or transfer: You are selling or transferring your business to another entity — old GSTIN must be cancelled, new owner needs a fresh GSTIN
  • Structure change: Converting from proprietorship to company or LLP — old GSTIN in individual name is cancelled, new GSTIN obtained in entity name
  • Cessation of taxable activity: You are stopping the specific activity for which GST was registered (e.g., selling goods) while continuing other non-taxable activities
  • Partnership dissolution: Partners parting ways — the firm's GSTIN must be cancelled and any continuing partner gets a fresh registration

5. GSTR-10 — The Final GST Return That Most Navsari Businesses Forget

GSTR-10 is mandatory after every GST cancellation — yet it is one of the most commonly missed requirements. Many Navsari businesses believe that cancelling the GST registration is the final step. It is not.

What GSTR-10 contains:

  • Details of all closing stock held at the time of cancellation
  • Input Tax Credit on that closing stock that must be reversed — this is a direct payment to the government
  • Details of goods in transit at the time of cancellation
  • Any capital goods held on which ITC was claimed — proportionate reversal required

GSTR-10 deadlines and penalties:

ScenarioGSTR-10 DeadlinePenalty for Late Filing
Voluntary cancellationWithin 3 months of cancellation effective date₹200/day — and cancellation remains pending
Department-initiated cancellationWithin 3 months of cancellation order date₹200/day — and department considers compliance incomplete

⚠️ A cancelled GSTIN where GSTR-10 has not been filed is legally still 'active' in the compliance sense. The GST portal continues to flag it as having pending compliance. This means all the risks of a dormant GSTIN continue even after cancellation — until GSTR-10 is filed. 6. Can You Revoke a GST Cancellation in Navsari?

Yes — cancellation is reversible under specific conditions:

  • Voluntary cancellation: Application for revocation can be filed within 30 days of the cancellation order — the GSTIN is restored to active status
  • Department-initiated cancellation: Revocation application can be filed within the appeal period specified in the cancellation order — typically 30 days
  • Condition for revocation: All pending returns must be filed and all outstanding dues cleared before revocation will be granted
  • After the revocation window: Fresh GST registration must be obtained — TaxAMC handles this as well
