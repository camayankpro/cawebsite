1. Who Must File ITR in Mangaluru?

  • Individuals: Annual income above ₹2.5 lakh (₹3 lakh for senior citizens, ₹5 lakh for super senior citizens)
  • Companies and LLPs: Mandatory filing regardless of profit or loss
  • Businesses with losses: Must file to carry forward business or capital losses to future years
  • Tax refund claimants: Anyone with TDS deducted in excess of actual tax liability
  • Foreign asset holders: Individuals holding foreign accounts, assets, or signing authority abroad
  • High-value transaction holders: Deposited ₹1 crore+ in bank accounts, spent ₹2 lakh+ on foreign travel, or paid ₹1 lakh+ in electricity bills in the year
  • Professionals and freelancers in Mangaluru earning via consultancy, rent, or business income

Even when not mandatory, filing ITR is strongly recommended for Mangaluru residents — it builds financial credibility for home loans, business loans, and visa applications.

3. Documents Required for ITR Filing in Mangaluru

  • For salaried individuals: Form 16 from employer, salary slips, bank statements, AIS / Form 26AS, investment proofs (80C, 80D, HRA, LTA)
  • For business owners: Profit & Loss account, Balance Sheet, GST returns, bank statements, TDS certificates (Form 16A)
  • For capital gains: Property sale deed, purchase agreement, capital gains computation statement, mutual fund statements
  • For rental income: Rent agreement, municipal tax receipts, home loan certificate
  • Common for all: PAN card, Aadhaar card, bank account details (for refund), AIS downloaded from income tax portal

7. Penalties for Late or Non-Filing

Missing the ITR deadline has serious financial consequences for Mangaluru taxpayers:

  • Late filing fee: ₹5,000 if filed after 31st July (₹1,000 if total income is below ₹5 lakh)
  • Interest under Section 234A: 1% per month on unpaid tax from the due date
  • Loss of carry-forward: Business losses, capital losses, and speculative losses cannot be carried forward if ITR is filed late
  • Prosecution: Wilful non-filing for high-income taxpayers can lead to prosecution under Section 276CC

⚠️ Important for Mangaluru taxpayers: The Annual Information Statement (AIS) captures all your financial transactions — bank interest, dividends, property sales, TDS, and more. The Income Tax Department cross-checks your ITR against AIS data. Discrepancies trigger notices. TaxAMC ensures your ITR accurately reflects all AIS data before filing.

8. Frequently Asked Questions — ITR Filing in Mangaluru

Q: What is the last date for ITR filing in Mangaluru?
For individuals and non-audit cases, the deadline is typically 31st July of the assessment year. Audit cases have a deadline of 31st October. Filing late attracts a fee of ₹5,000 under Section 234F.

Q: Which ITR form should I use for my business in Mangaluru?
Business owners typically use ITR-3 or ITR-4 (for presumptive taxation). Our CA team will identify the correct form based on your income type and business structure in Mangaluru.

Q: Is it compulsory to file ITR if income is below the taxable limit?
Not mandatory for most. However, filing is strongly recommended to claim refunds, build a financial track record, and carry forward losses.

Q: Can TaxAMC handle income tax notices for Mangaluru taxpayers?
Yes. We handle all notices — scrutiny assessments, demand notices, refund queries, and reassessment under Section 148 — for businesses and individuals in Mangaluru.

Q: What is the difference between old and new tax regime for Mangaluru taxpayers?
The old regime allows deductions (80C, HRA, etc.) while the new regime offers lower slab rates without most deductions. Our CA team compares both regimes and recommends the one that saves more tax for your specific situation.
