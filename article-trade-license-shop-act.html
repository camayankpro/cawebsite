1. What is Trade License / Shop Act Registration?

Shop Act Registration (also called Trade License or Gumasta License in some states) is a legal requirement under the Uttar Pradesh Shops and Commercial Establishments Act. It is a government certificate that:

  • Legally authorises you to operate a commercial establishment at the registered address
  • Regulates working hours, holidays, leave entitlements, and employment conditions for employees
  • Is required to open a current bank account for most private and nationalised banks in UP
  • Is required for other registrations like GST, FSSAI, and professional tax
  • Is required for renewal of lease agreements and commercial property

2. Who Needs to Register Under Shop Act in UP?

Registration is required for all commercial establishments in Uttar Pradesh, including:

  • Retail and wholesale shops
  • Restaurants, hotels, dhabas, bakeries, and food establishments
  • Trading offices, service centres, and showrooms
  • Private companies, LLPs, and partnership firms operating offices
  • Factories not covered under the Factories Act
  • Professionals like CAs, doctors, lawyers operating offices
  • Home-based businesses with employees or commercial activity

Establishments employing even one worker (other than family members) are required to register. Many businesses register even without employees â€” as it is needed for bank accounts and other compliance.

3. Documents Required for Shop Act Registration in UP

  • Identity proof of proprietor/partners/directors (Aadhaar, PAN)
  • Address proof of the business premises (electricity bill, rent agreement, property tax receipt)
  • Rent agreement (if rented premises)
  • Photograph of the owner/authorised signatory
  • PAN card of the business/individual
  • Nature of business description
  • Number of employees (can be zero for sole proprietors)
