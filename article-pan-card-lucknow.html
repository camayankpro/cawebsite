1. What is PAN and Why it Matters in Lucknow

PAN (Permanent Account Number) is a 10-digit alphanumeric identifier issued by the Income Tax Department. It is mandatory for: filing ITR, opening bank accounts, buying or selling property above ₹10 lakh, fixed deposits above ₹50,000, GST registration, company incorporation, and most financial transactions above specified limits.

In Lucknow, with its active Chikankari embroidery and textile crafts and Government and PSU contracting sectors, businesses and individuals regularly encounter PAN requirements — for vendor registrations, banking, and regulatory filings. An invalid or inoperative PAN can block critical transactions.

2. How to Apply for PAN Card Online from Lucknow

PAN applications are fully online — residents and businesses in Lucknow can apply without visiting any office:

  • NSDL (Protean): onlineservices.nsdl.com → Services → PAN → Apply Online → Form 49A (Indian citizens)
  • UTI Infrastructure: pan.utiitsl.com → Apply for New PAN

Fee: ₹107 for delivery within India (including Lucknow). PAN card delivered within 15–20 working days. e-PAN is available instantly after processing.

6. PAN-Aadhaar Linking — Mandatory for Lucknow Residents

All individuals in Lucknow (except exempt categories) must link their PAN with Aadhaar. Unlinked PANs are inoperative — consequences include:

  • Cannot file ITR or receive tax refunds
  • TDS deducted at higher rate of 20% instead of standard rates
  • Cannot open new bank accounts or demat accounts
  • Critical financial transactions get blocked

How to link from Lucknow: incometax.gov.in → Link Aadhaar → Enter PAN and Aadhaar → Pay ₹1,000 penalty fee → OTP verification. Status updates within 4–7 working days.
