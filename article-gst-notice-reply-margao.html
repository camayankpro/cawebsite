1. Received a GST Notice in Margao? Do This Immediately

Step 1 — Don't panic, but act now. Every GST notice has a strict deadline — 7 to 30 days. The clock starts from the date on the notice, not from when you read it.

Step 2 — Do not ignore it. This is the most dangerous mistake. An unanswered notice results in an ex-parte order — the GST officer issues a demand based solely on the department's calculation. You lose the right to present your explanation or evidence. Ex-parte orders typically carry:

  • Full tax demand as per the department's computation
  • 18% per annum interest from the original due date
  • 100% penalty on the tax demand in cases of suppression or misrepresentation
  • Bank account attachment for recovery — no further notice required

3. DRC-01 — The Most Critical GST Notice Your Margao Business Can Receive

The DRC-01 (Show Cause Notice cum Demand) is the most consequential GST notice — it means the department believes you owe additional tax and gives you a formal opportunity to explain why you don't, or settle the amount if you do. Every DRC-01 requires a detailed, professionally drafted DRC-06 reply with supporting documentation and legal arguments.

Most common reasons DRC-01 is issued to Margao businesses:

  • ITC on invoices not in GSTR-2B: You claimed ITC on supplier invoices that the supplier never filed — the most common DRC-01 trigger
  • Turnover mismatch: Sales declared in GSTR-1 are lower than turnover in your ITR or Form 26AS/AIS — department demands tax on the difference
  • ITC on blocked categories: Cars, food, personal expenses claimed as ITC — specifically identified and demanded back
  • Wrong HSN code — lower rate applied: You applied 5% when 12% or 18% was correct — department raises demand for the difference
  • RCM (Reverse Charge Mechanism) liability not paid: Certain services require you to pay GST as the recipient — commonly missed for legal services, security, GTA transport
  • Export invoices without LUT and IGST: Exported goods/services without filing LUT and without paying IGST — non-compliant exports attract demand
  • Fake invoice ITC: ITC claimed on invoices from suppliers who are non-existent or have been flagged by the department as bogus bill issuers

TaxAMC's DRC-01 response process for Margao businesses:

  • Demand analysis: We review the notice in detail — separating what is correct, what is overstated, and what is entirely disputable
  • Evidence gathering: Purchase invoices, bank statements, contracts, correspondence — every document that supports your position collected and organised
  • Legal research: Applicable GST circulars, notifications, court judgments, and Advance Authority Rulings (AAR) that support your case identified and cited
  • DRC-06 reply drafting: Comprehensive reply filed on the GST portal using precise legal language, supported by all evidence and relevant legal authorities
  • Partial payment strategy: If part of the demand is valid, we advise on making a partial payment to reduce penalty exposure while disputing the remainder
  • Personal hearing: If required, TaxAMC CA represents your business at the personal hearing before the GST officer

4. ITC Mismatch Notices — The Most Common GST Notice in Margao

ITC mismatch notices are sent by the GST portal automatically when your ITC claims in GSTR-3B exceed or don't match the ITC reflected in your GSTR-2B. These are the most widespread GST notices across all Margao businesses — and also the most commonly mishandled.

Why ITC mismatches happen:

  • Your supplier filed their GSTR-1 late — your ITC appeared in GSTR-2B after you already claimed it in GSTR-3B
  • Your supplier made an error in their GSTR-1 — wrong GSTIN, wrong invoice number, wrong amount
  • Your supplier is registered in one state but filed the invoice under a different GSTIN
  • You received a physical invoice but your supplier hasn't filed their return at all
  • You claimed ITC in a month before the supplier's return was filed (timing difference)

TaxAMC's ITC mismatch response approach:

  • Reconciliation analysis: We compare your purchase register against GSTR-2B data — identifying every mismatch and its root cause
  • Supplier categorisation: Mismatches are categorised as: timing differences (supplier filed late), supplier errors (wrong data), supplier defaults (not filed), and genuine discrepancies
  • Response strategy: For timing mismatches — prove ITC is now reflected in later GSTR-2B. For supplier errors — provide purchase invoice and proof of payment. For supplier defaults — demonstrate bona fide purchase and advise on supplier follow-up
  • Voluntary ITC reversal: Where ITC is genuinely not recoverable (supplier will never file), we advise on voluntary reversal with interest — which is less than the penalty a contested demand carries
